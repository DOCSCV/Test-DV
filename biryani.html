A Glimpse into Eastern Cuisine

From the fragrant spices of Biryani to the tangy zest of Chicken 65, Eastern cuisine offers a symphony of flavors that tantalize the taste buds. Chicken Tikka Masala, with its creamy tomato sauce, and Chicken Korma, a rich and nutty delight, are just a few examples of the culinary treasures you'll discover.

Join me as we delve into the art of creating these delectable dishes, step-by-step. Whether you're a seasoned chef or a curious beginner, I invite you to embark on this flavorful adventure and experience the magic of Eastern cooking.

Click below to see the recipe!

  • Biryani: A fragrant rice dish layered with marinated meat (like chicken, lamb, or mutton), vegetables, and a medley of aromatic spices such as saffron, cardamom, and cinnamon. It's a celebratory dish known for its exquisite aroma and complex flavors, often served with raita (a yogurt-based condiment) and salan (a savory curry).
  • Chicken 65: A fiery and flavorful appetizer originating from Chennai, India. This dish features bite-sized pieces of chicken marinated in a blend of yogurt, ginger, garlic, and a generous dose of chili powder. The chicken is then deep-fried to a crispy perfection and often served with a tangy dipping sauce.
  • Chicken Tikka
    • Masala: A beloved classic that showcases tender pieces of chicken tikka (marinated and grilled chicken) simmered in a creamy tomato-based gravy. The sauce is enriched with butter, onions, garlic, ginger, and a blend of aromatic spices, creating a harmonious balance of flavors.
  • Chicken Korma: A decadent and indulgent curry with origins in Mughlai cuisine. This dish features tender chicken cooked in a rich and creamy sauce made with yogurt, nuts (like almonds and cashews), and a delicate blend of spices. The korma is known for its velvety texture and subtle sweetness, offering a luxurious culinary experience.
